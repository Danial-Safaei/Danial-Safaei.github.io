About

I'm a PhD researcher at WMG, University of Warwick, working on Safe AI and Autonomous Driving Systems in collaboration with Siemens.

My work focuses on ensuring safe deployment of AI in safety-critical autonomous systems through synthetic data fidelity assessment, scenario-based testing, and verification & validation methodologies.

With a background in Computer Science and a Master's in Artificial Intelligence specializing in Reinforcement Learning, I bring software engineering experience to research in computer vision, trajectory prediction, and machine learning for smart systems.

For metrics and full publication lists, see Google Scholar, ResearchGate, arXiv.

Research

  • Safe AI & V&V for autonomous systems. Building frameworks for responsible deployment of AI in safety-critical driving stacks.
  • Scenario-based testing. Contributing to Safety Pool™ and developing scenario generation and coverage methodologies.
  • Synthetic data fidelity. Evaluating realism requirements for perception and downstream decision-making.
  • Perception & trajectory prediction. Applying computer vision and inverse reinforcement learning in smart environments.
