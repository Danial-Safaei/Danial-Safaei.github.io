About

Danial Safaei is a PhD researcher at WMG, University of Warwick, where he investigates the foundations of safe and trustworthy AI for autonomous systems.

His research bridges three disciplines: formal safety assurance, scenario-based testing methodologies, and the fidelity of synthetic data for model validation. This cross-domain perspective has produced work spanning smart environments, biomedical AI, and autonomous vehicle safety — unified by a commitment to rigorous, deployable, and explainable AI systems.

The Decisive Feature Fidelity (DFF) framework — his most recent contribution — introduces a principled metric for quantifying how well synthetic imagery preserves the features that drive model decisions, addressing a critical gap in simulation-to-real transfer research.

Research Focus

Three intersecting domains that define an agenda for trustworthy autonomous intelligence.

Primary

Safe AI for Autonomous Systems

Developing formal safety assurance methods and trustworthy deployment architectures for AI in high-stakes autonomous contexts — where failure carries real-world consequence and verification is non-negotiable.

Primary

Scenario-Based Testing

Rigorous methodologies for scenario generation, structured evaluation, and edge-case validation — ensuring autonomous systems are stress-tested against the full distribution of real-world operating conditions.

Primary

Synthetic Data Fidelity

Principled quantification of how synthetic and real imagery align at the feature level — answering whether a model trained on simulation will behave reliably in the real world.
