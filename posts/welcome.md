Welcome to the blog. I'm **Danial Safaei**, a PhD researcher at WMG, University of Warwick, working on Safe AI for autonomous systems.

I built this space to write in the open — short, technical pieces that sit somewhere between a lab notebook and a proper article.

## What I'll write about

- **Safe AI** — how we argue, with evidence, that an intelligent system is trustworthy enough to deploy.
- **Evaluation methodology** — testing, scenario design, and the metrics that actually track what we care about.
- **Synthetic-data fidelity** — when simulated data can stand in for the real world, and when it quietly can't.
- **The research process** — the messy, honest parts: dead ends, surprises, and things I changed my mind about.

## Why bother writing

Research papers are compressed. A lot of the useful thinking — the "why this and not that" — never makes it into the final PDF. Writing here is a way to keep those threads, and hopefully to be useful to someone facing the same problem.

> Good evaluation is not about making a system look impressive. It's about knowing, precisely, where it can be trusted and where it can't.
